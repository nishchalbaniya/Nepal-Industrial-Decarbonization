### A.2 Engagement structure

| Stage | Description | Timeline | Fee (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1: Pre-validation gap analysis** | Desktop review of the PDD and supporting documentation. Issue a written gap analysis report identifying any missing or weak sections. | 10 working days from receipt of complete pack | $5,000–$8,000 |
| **Stage 2: Full validation** | Detailed validation per ISO 14064-3:2019 and VCS Standard v4.6, including one site visit to PlantA. Issue draft Validation Report, address CARs/CLs, issue final Validation Report. | 60 working days from end of Stage 1 | $20,000–$30,000 |
| **Stage 3: First verification (separate engagement)** | Verification of the first monitoring report and issuance request. | 30 working days | $12,000–$18,000 |
| **Stage 4: Subsequent verifications (5 cycles, separate engagements)** | Verification of monitoring reports for monitoring years 2–10. | 30 working days each | $12,000–$18,000 each |

**Indicative total Stage 1 + Stage 2 fee: USD $25,000–$38,000** (excluding travel, accommodation, and any data room subscription).

### A.3 Validation scope (Stage 2)

The validation shall cover, at minimum, the following PDD sections and assessments:

1. **Project description** (PDD §A) — project boundary, equipment, gases, project activities
2. **Baseline scenario** (PDD §B) — alternative analysis, common practice, emissions trajectory
3. **Additionality** (PDD §C) — investment analysis, barrier test, common practice, prior consideration, regulatory surplus
4. **Leakage** (PDD §D) — per-activity assessment, quantitative estimate
5. **Emission reductions** (PDD §E) — gross / leakage / buffer / net, uncertainty
6. **Monitoring plan** (PDD §F) — parameter table, frequencies, QA/QC, data archive
7. **Stakeholder consultation** (PDD §G) — minutes, attendance, photos, grievance mechanism
8. **GHG information system** — data flow, audit trail, integrity
9. **Materiality threshold** — 5% of gross reductions, justified
10. **GWP and emission factor sources** — dated, citable, authoritative
11. **VCS Standard v4.6 compliance** — including prior consideration, public funding, double counting
12. **Methodology compliance** — AMS-III.H and ACM0010 specific requirements

| 1 | Gap analysis report | PDF | 10 WD from kickoff |
| 2 | Draft Validation Report | DOCX | 30 WD from end of Stage 1 |

### A.5 VVB team

TÜV SÜD shall assign a team with the following minimum competencies (per ISO 14064-3:2019 §5.4 and VCS Standard v4.6):

- **Lead validator:** 8+ years of GHG validation experience, 20+ completed VCS validations, cement or heavy industry expertise, ISO 14064-3 lead validator certification
- **Technical reviewer:** 5+ years of cement industry experience, process engineering background
- **Local reviewer (Nepal):** 3+ years of Nepali environmental regulatory experience, Nepali language capability, ISO 14001 lead auditor certification preferred
- **Independent reviewer:** Internal TÜV person, not involved in the engagement, for the 4-eyes principle

CVs and independence statements for the entire team shall be provided with the proposal. The lead validator shall not have worked on any competing HCN or rival project in the previous 24 months.

### A.6 Methodology and standards

The validation shall be performed in accordance with:
- ISO 14064-3:2019 — Specification with guidance for the verification and validation of GHG statements
- Verra VCS Standard v4.6
- Verra AMS-III.H v1.0 (for the biomass co-firing component)
- Verra ACM0010 v3.0 (for the WHR component)
- Verra Tool01 (Combined tool for identification of baselines and demonstration of additionality)
- Verra Tool07 (Workbook calculation of project emissions)
- Verra Tool10 (Default values)

### A.7 Materiality threshold

The materiality threshold for the validation shall be **5% of the gross annual emission reductions** (e.g., for PlantA: 5% × 70,000 = 3,500 tCO₂/yr). Misstatements below this threshold are aggregated and reported as a single item. Misstatements above this threshold require individual resolution.

### A.8 Site visit

A 2-day site visit to PlantA Industries Ltd is mandatory. TÜV SÜD shall:
- Walk the plant perimeter
- Inspect the kilns, preheater, and (where constructed) the WHR and biomass systems
- Interview plant management, chief engineer, EHS officer, and HCN project team
- Spot-check 5+ randomly-selected monitoring parameters against source documents
- Hold an open findings meeting at the end of the visit

HCN shall provide:
- Travel and accommodation (PlantA is 4–5 hours by road from Kathmandu; nearest airport is Simara, then 1 hour by road)
- Plant access, escort, and safety PPE
- Conference room and document review facilities
- 1-day pre-visit document review (recommended)

## SCHEDULE E — CONFIDENTIALITY AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### E.1 Confidentiality

Each party shall keep confidential all information disclosed by the other party in connection with this engagement, except:
- Information that is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party
- Information that is independently developed by the receiving party
- Information that is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order
- Information disclosed to Verra as part of the validation pipeline (TÜV SÜD's VR is publicly available on the Verra registry)

Confidentiality obligations survive for 5 years post-completion.

### E.2 Intellectual property

- HCN retains all IP in the PDD, calculations, and supporting documentation
- TÜV SÜD retains IP in the Validation Report and any proprietary validation tools
- TÜV SÜD may reference the engagement in its client list, with HCN's prior approval
- HCN may reference the Validation Report in its marketing materials and investor briefings

### E.3 Audit trail access

TÜV SÜD shall have read-only access to the HCN audit trail database (SQLite dump, monthly) for the duration of the engagement and 2 years post-completion. HCN shall not modify or delete any audit trail entry post-engagement-completion without TÜV SÜD's written approval.

## SCHEDULE F — LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### F.1 TÜV SÜD liability

TÜV SÜD's total aggregate liability under this engagement shall not exceed USD $100,000 or the total fees paid under this engagement, whichever is greater. TÜV SÜD shall maintain professional indemnity insurance of at least USD $5 million for the duration of the engagement and 5 years post-completion.

### F.2 HCN liability

HCN's total aggregate liability under this engagement shall not exceed USD $100,000 or the total fees paid under this engagement, whichever is greater.

### F.3 Indemnification

Each party shall indemnify the other against third-party claims arising from its own negligence or willful misconduct in the performance of this engagement, subject to the liability caps in F.1 and F.2.

### F.4 Force majeure

Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform due to force majeure events (war, civil unrest, pandemic, natural disaster, government action), provided that the affected party notifies the other within 7 days and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

## SCHEDULE G — TERMINATION

### G.1 Termination for convenience

Either party may terminate this engagement for convenience with 30 days' written notice. HCN shall pay for all work performed up to the termination date, plus any non-cancellable expenses.

### G.2 Termination for cause

Either party may terminate this engagement for cause (material breach, insolvency, change of control) with 14 days' written notice, if the breach is not cured within the notice period. The terminating party is not liable for fees beyond the termination date.

### G.3 Termination by HCN for VVB non-performance

If TÜV SÜD fails to deliver the Stage 2 draft VR within 60 working days of Stage 2 kickoff, HCN may terminate with 14 days' notice. HCN shall pay for work performed, but TÜV SÜD shall reimburse HCN for any reasonable costs incurred to engage a replacement VVB, capped at 20% of the Stage 2 fee.
