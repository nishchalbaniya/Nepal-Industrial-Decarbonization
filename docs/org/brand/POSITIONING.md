Ten words. Verb-led. Outcome-led. Free is the hook. If a PlantA plant manager
remembers nothing else, they should remember this sentence.

## Why us, why now

**The founder story.** Nishchal Baniya is a Nepali engineer who got tired
of watching Nepal's cement and brick industry fly blind on emissions while
European, Chinese, and Indian competitors were already reporting and
monetising theirs. He built `nepal_decarb_pro` himself — an open-source
(MIT) platform that scored **9.78/10 against international standards**,
with **78/78 tests passing** and a **live demo running in Kathmandu**. No
foreign co-founder, no VC money, no offshore team. The proof is in the
PlantA pilot: a real plant, real data, real numbers.

**The market timing.** Three forces are hitting Nepali industry at once in
2026–2027: (1) **EU CBAM** is in its definitive phase — imported cement
into the EU now pays a carbon tariff based on its embedded emissions; (2)
**Nepal's NDC** commits to net-zero by 2045 and the Department of
Environment needs an MRV system to track it; (3) **International banks**
are requiring PCAF-aligned emissions disclosure before lending to
industrial clients. The first Nepali plant to be ready for all three
wins the next decade of contracts. None of them are ready today. We are.

## Who we are NOT for (be specific, protect the brand)

- **European or North American cement companies.** They have Watershed,
  Persefoni, Sphera, and the budget to use them. We don't.
- **Chinese cement companies.** They have China ETS, China CCER, and
  their own tools. We don't serve them.
- **Cement traders, brokers, and commodity houses.** We measure at the
  kiln, not in a shipping container.
- **Carbon credit speculators with no real project.** We are an MRV
  platform, not a credit trading desk.
- **Steel, fertilizer, chemical, or aluminium plants.** We are
  cement-and-brick-specific. Not a generic GHG tool.
- **White-label consulting shops looking to rebrand our work.** We are
  open-source. Use the code. Don't resell it.
- **Anyone looking for a "we'll do it for you" full-service agency.** We
  build the platform and train your team. We don't run your plant.

## Voice & tone (5 adjectives, internal rules)

1. **Technical-but-warm.** Cite the standard, name the equation, then
   translate it into what it means for the plant manager's job.
2. **Specific-not-vague.** "861,025 tCO₂/yr at PlantA", not
   "millions of tonnes". Every claim has a number or a name.
3. **Bilingual-first.** Every English sentence is paired with a Nepali
   version written by a Nepali domain expert — never machine-translated.
   The default language of the platform is the user's choice.
4. **No-greenwashing.** We publish the bad numbers too. PlantA is
   **12% above the GCCA global benchmark** at 783 kg CO₂/t cement. We
   say so. Trust is the asset.
5. **Nepal-first.** Built in Kathmandu, by a Nepali engineer, for Nepali
   plants. We don't apologise for that.

**Words we use freely:** baseload, NEA grid, kiln, calcination,
clinker-to-cement ratio, Verra, ISO 14064, PDD, EU CBAM, bankable,
audit-ready, टोली (team), सहयोग (collaboration).

**Words we never use:** synergy, leverage, unlock, disrupt, paradigm,
best-in-class, world-leading, "empower", "delight", "next-gen",
"revolutionary". If a sentence needs any of these words, the sentence
needs to be rewritten.
