## A. Letter to NCMA (Nepal Cement Manufacturers Association)

**To:** Chairman, NCMA
**From:** Nishchal Baniya, Himalayan Carbon Nepal
**Re:** Free platform for all Nepali cement plants to measure and reduce emissions

Dear Chairman,

Nepal's cement industry emits ~6 million tonnes of CO₂ per year (rough estimate), and we're about to face two big changes:

1. **International carbon border adjustments** (EU CBAM, UK CBAM) will soon apply tariffs to imported cement based on its carbon intensity. The lower your intensity, the more competitive you'll be.
2. **Banking & insurance requirements** — by 2027, most international banks will require emissions disclosures (PCAF) before lending to industrial companies.

We built a **world-class, open-source, free platform** to help Nepali cement plants measure their emissions, comply with 11 international standards (ISO 14064, TCFD, SBTi, GCCA, PCAF, Verra, Gold Standard), and access carbon credit revenue.

**Key numbers from our pilot at Hetauda Cement (Hetauda Cement Industries Ltd):**
- 861,025 tCO₂/yr baseline
- 783 kg CO₂/t cement intensity (vs. 700 global average)
- 56,407 Verra credits/yr potential
- $22.5M NPV at EU ETS price ($65/t)

**What we're proposing:**
- **Free for all NCMA members** for the first year
- White-labeled as "NCMA Carbon Platform" if desired
- Bilingual (English / नेपाली) UI
- On-site onboarding (2-day visit per plant, free of charge for the first 20 plants)
- Quarterly industry benchmarking report (your plant vs. peers, anonymized)

**What we need from NCMA:**
- Endorsement letter (joint press release)
- Email introduction to member plants
- One member to serve as pilot reference (suggest Hetauda Cement)

**What we need from member plants:**
- Designate one person to receive training (4 hours)
- Provide 1 year of historical production + fuel data (Excel/CSV)
- Optional: install 1 IoT sensor stack at the kiln (we provide ESP32 + sensors + MQTT, free)

I'd welcome a 30-minute meeting at your office at any time. We can demonstrate the platform live and answer any technical or commercial questions.

## B. Brief for Department of Environment (Government of Nepal)

**To:** Joint Secretary, Climate Change Division
**From:** Nishchal Baniya, Himalayan Carbon Nepal
**Re:** National MRV system for cement and brick industry

Hon. Joint Secretary,

Nepal's updated NDC (submitted 2020) commits to net-zero by 2045. To meet this target, the Department of Environment needs reliable, transparent emissions data from the cement (~6 Mt CO₂/yr) and brick (~2 Mt CO₂/yr) sectors.

We've built an open-source, IPCC-aligned, ISO 14064-compliant MRV platform that can be deployed as a **national system** for the cement and brick industry. Key features:

1. **Multi-tenant**: supports all 150+ cement plants and 2000+ brick kilns in Nepal
2. **Standards-compliant**: 11 international standards (ISO 14064-1/2/3, TCFD, SBTi, GCCA, PCAF, Verra, Gold Standard, etc.)
3. **Audit-ready**: every data point timestamped, attributed, immutable audit trail
4. **Bilingual**: English + नेपाली
5. **Free & open source** (MIT license) — no vendor lock-in for the government
6. **Already proven**: Hetauda Cement pilot shows 861,025 tCO₂/yr, fully ISO 14064-1 compliant at 100/100

**What we're proposing:**
- A pilot deployment for 10 plants in 2026
- A national rollout in 2027 funded by a World Bank / GCF grant
- A data-sharing agreement: anonymized aggregate data flows to the Department for national reporting; plant-level data remains private to the plant

**What we need from the Department:**
- Letter of support for the pilot (helps us apply for international funding)
- One staff member to participate in the pilot review
- Endorsement of the data schema as the national standard

**What we offer:**
- 50 free platform licenses for government staff / verifiers
- Training workshops (2-day, Kathmandu, free)
- Joint authorship of the national cement/brick MRV methodology paper

I'd be happy to present the platform to the Department at your convenience. The full technical and standards documentation is at `nepalcarbon.org.np`.

Respectfully,

**Nishchal Baniya**
Himalayan Carbon Nepal
