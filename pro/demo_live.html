nepal_decarb_pro -- honest status, 2026-07-23

Open-source industrial decarbonization toolkit for Nepal's cement and brick industry. v1.0 sizing model.

Read this first

The previous version of this page contained false claims: a 9.78/10 rating, 78/78 tests, $22.5M NPV, "Verified on PlantA", a fictional "VM0009 v2.0 cement methodology", and a Nepal grid emission factor that was wrong by 4 orders of magnitude. Those have been removed.

This is now a sizing model, not a submittable carbon credit. The engineering simulators (cooler, kiln, brick, LCA) produce real physics output. The standards and markets modules (ISO 14064, Verra, Gold Standard, SBTi) are check-wrappers, not compliance engines. The PDD generator is a sizing tool, not a submittable PDD.

The full remediation report is at reviews/REMEDIATION_REPORT.md. The honest standards coverage is at docs/STANDARDS_COVERAGE.md. The honest methodology is at docs/METHODOLOGY.md.

What is removed (was on this page before)

Removed 2026-07-23 in the WP0-WP6 credibility remediation pass:

  • The 9.78/10 self-awarded rating
  • The "5-axis rating, every axis >= 95/100" table
  • The "78/78 tests passing" badge
  • The "$22.5M NPV at EU ETS $65/t" claim
  • The "Verified on PlantA Industries Ltd" framing (no VVB sign-off)
  • The fictional "VM0009 v2.0 (Cement Plant Decarbonization)" methodology citation (real VM0009 is AFOLU grasslands)
  • The TPDDTEC-for-cement citation (TPDDTEC is for cookstoves, <= 150 kW/unit)
  • The "SBTi: 1.5C aligned (56% reduction vs 38% required)" claim (the pathway multipliers were hard-coded, not SBTi-validated)
  • The "ISO 14064-1: 100/100 (20/20 criteria met)" claim (the checker is real, but the 100% is self-asserted)
  • The "NEA grid EF 0.0256 kg CO2/kWh" figure (the real value from IEA/Enerdata 2024 is ~0.0023 g/kWh -- off by 4 orders of magnitude)
  • The "$30/hr GPU 24/7" recommendation (now correctly labelled as optional, not required)
