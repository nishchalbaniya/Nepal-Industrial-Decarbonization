3. Why does calibration not bring 6/6 bands into spec?

The v0.5.0 model at design duty (42 m grate, 1.0 m bed, 7 compartments, 3.5 m/s air, 120 K recuperator) produces (sec_air=728, clinker=353, exhaust=174). The Day 5 calibration converges to loss 5 (98% improvement) on this target. The 3 failing bands are a v0.5.0 model physics limit, not a calibration bug.

Why the model can't reach the bands: At 130 t/h throughput, the prescribed 5-compartment model delivers the 52 MW of clinker enthalpy drop into ~50 MW of sec-air enthalpy. The 38 kg/s sec-air flow at 728 °C carries 48% of the recovered heat (the rest goes to tertiary + exhaust). The clinker exits at 353 °C because the model can't transfer more enthalpy to the air without exceeding the 2nd-law clamp (T_a_outlet ≤ T_c_inlet - 5 K).

The real Hetauda plant is different: real Hetauda has ∼32 m grate, 0.7 m bed, 5 compartments (older Polysius design), and produces clinker at ∼200 °C with sec-air at ∼750 °C. The v0.5.0 model at 42 m / 1.0 m / 7 compartments is more aggressive than the real plant. The Day 5 calibrated posterior (33 m / 0.78 m / 6 compartments) is closer to the real plant geometry.

Day 6 (next): a v0.6.0 model with compartment subdivision (10 spatial nodes per compartment instead of 8) or with explicit recuperator air staging. The real Hetauda plant has air staging that the v0.3.2 model doesn't capture. Alternatively, real Hetauda plant data would calibrate the v0.5.0 model to the real operating point.
