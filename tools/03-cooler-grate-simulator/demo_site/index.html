6. Why does calibration not bring 7/7 bands into spec?

The optimizer is genuinely doing its job — sec-air 798 °C, efficiency 0.73, first-law 0.0 are all in band. The clinker-outlet band [120, 200] °C is the constraint the v0.3.2 model cannot satisfy at the 130 t/h Hetauda throughput with the prescribed 5-compartment × 28 m × 1.5 m/s geometry.

Root cause: the prescribed geometry has 5 compartments × 28 m = 140 m² of grate area, with 38 kg/s sec-air. The enthalpy drop on the clinker side is ~52 MW, but the air-side capacity at this geometry is ~1310 K air-side ΔT maximum (Achenbach 1995 + Mujumdar 2007 §2.2). At the design duty the air side can pick up ~50 MW but the clinker exits at 397 °C instead of 150 °C — the heat transfer area is too small to reach the design clinker-outlet.

Day 5 fix: introduce operating-handle freedom that the v0.3.2 model doesn't have. Three options: (a) longer grate (35 m or 42 m, not 28 m), (b) deeper bed (1.0 m, not 0.7 m), (c) higher heat-transfer coefficient via a finer compartment subdivision (10 compartments, not 5). The real Hetauda plant has ~32 m grate and 0.7 m bed; option (c) is the closest match to plant reality and the Day 5 spec.
